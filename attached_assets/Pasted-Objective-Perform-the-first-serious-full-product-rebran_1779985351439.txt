Objective:
Perform the first serious full-product rebrand sprint from Alpenvia to Josephine.

This is not a simple rename. Reposition the app from a heavy hiking/adventure platform into a warm, premium, human alpine companion that helps people decide what beautiful mountain day to have in South Tyrol, Trentino, the Dolomites and nearby alpine regions.

Product direction:
Josephine is a friendly local alpine companion, crafted in the heart of South Tyrol, Italy.

She helps users answer:
“What kind of alpine day should I have today?”

The app should feel alive, curated, local, emotional and useful.
It should feel locally born, not generic.
The South Tyrol origin is part of the brand trust and emotional identity.

The app should not feel like AllTrails, Komoot, Outdooractive, Strava or a generic route database.

Target users:
- Tourists visiting South Tyrol / Trentino / Dolomites
- Couples looking for a beautiful day
- Families
- Dog owners
- Older visitors who want simple, clear suggestions
- Casual hikers
- Locals looking for curated weekend ideas

Core feeling:
Premium, warm, calm, local, elegant, trustworthy.
Less “performance app”.
More “local guide who knows the mountains”.

Important:
Do not create a cheap mascot.
Do not create a childish CSS avatar.
Do not use primitive circles/faces as a fake 3D character.
Josephine should feel premium, cinematic and human.

If no final 3D mascot/guide asset exists, create an elegant mascot-ready hero area with a polished guide card / visual placeholder that clearly expects a future premium illustration or 3D render.
It must look intentional, premium and finished enough for a first rebrand sprint — not unfinished, childish or cheap.
